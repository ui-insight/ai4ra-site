Early conversations around AI4RA consistently return to a few operational realities.

First, institutions want interoperability, but they experience it as staff time,
translation effort, and local reporting constraints rather than as an abstract
architecture goal.

Second, AI interest is real, but practitioners want transparent boundaries. They want to
know what a system is doing, what data it depends on, and where human review remains
essential.

Third, communities of practice matter because institutions rarely need entirely novel
answers. They need evidence that peers have navigated similar governance, data, and
workflow issues successfully.

Field notes should capture this kind of grounded observation so the site feels alive and
useful, not merely aspirational.
