Body:

The AI4RA community has been clear about what it needs most: not more AI hype, but real
relief from repetitive, brittle, high-stakes work.

Our new field note, *From Community Pain Points to Product Fit*, draws on the March 2026
thematic analysis of 160 substantive community responses and asks a simple question: how do
those needs align with the tools we are building?

The piece argues that Vandalizer and OpenERA address different parts of the same workflow
problem. Vandalizer is the governed workflow layer for bounded, testable AI tasks such as
document extraction, first-pass analysis, and review support. OpenERA is the operational
platform layer where proposal development, review routing, compliance tracking, and related
institutional workflows can happen in an inspectable open system.

The field note also stays precise about what is already usable, what is emerging, and where
the community's feedback points to the next highest-value work.

If you have been trying to explain how AI4RA moves from community pain points to practical
infrastructure, this is a good place to start.
